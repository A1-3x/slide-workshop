Part 1: Stay Focused

"Effective design should start with a visual task analysis, determine the set of visual queries to be supported by a design, and then use color, form, and space to efficiently serve those queries."

Visual Thinking for Design

Colin Ware

Part 1: Stay Focused

  • Include only one idea per slide
  • Make objects appear only when mentioned.
  • Dim objects after they're discussed.
  • Use no more than 4 bullets per slide.

Part 2: Pretty Pictures

  • Use images instead of text when possible.
  • Use high-resolution, royalty-free images.

Source: US National Archives

Part 3: Accessibility

  • Use large font - at least 24 points
  • Avoid pointless animations
  • Use alt text for images, especially if distributing slides

Contemporary Considerations

  • You may need to perform live demonstration (web application, data dashboard, game, etc.)
    • Avoid exiting slideshow to switch windows
    • Use Iframes, which embed a website into your slides

Contemporary Considerations

  • Audience may ask for slides to read/reference later
    • Publish online (GitHub, Google Docs, personal site, etc.)
    • Should be easy to reach
      • Link on last slide
      • QR code(s)
    • Use as opportunity to include additional information
